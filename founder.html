From Moscow to the Lancet — and rural Michigan

Sergey trained as a physician in Russia, earned his PhD in epidemiology from the Russian Research Institute of Public Health (Ministry of Health), then built a research career spanning the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, the European Office of the WHO, and the Frankfurt University of Applied Sciences. A Fulbright Scholarship brought him to Central Michigan University in 2011 — where he worked with Professor Carl Lee building mathematical models of disease. He eventually returned for good.

As a Global Burden of Disease collaborator with IHME, his work appeared in The Lancet Neurology, Allergy, and was cited by The Economist. When Russia invaded Ukraine, Sergey chose to leave — continuing his research from Uzbekistan and then permanently at CMU, where he now runs the Lakes Linked project: applying GBD methodology at the county level for the first time.
